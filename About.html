Our mission

Christian Steps Ministries (CSM) is a ministry dedicated to assisting any and all people who find themselves on a journey to pursue a life of following Jesus Christ. We learn through the Bible that Jesus is the one true Son of God and that we are to worship Him and follow His teachings in our lives.

  • Encourage hearts with Christ-centered hope.
  • Strengthen faith through Scripture-based teaching.
  • Offer practical steps that help you live what you believe.

Who this is for

Christian Steps is for:

  • People who are curious about Jesus and want a simple place to begin.
  • Believers who want encouragement and practical teaching.
  • Anyone walking through hardship who needs hope and prayer.
  • Those who feel stuck and need a gentle “next step” forward.

No matter your background, you can start with one small step: listen, read, pray, and reach out.

Our values

Scripture first

We want every teaching to point back to God’s Word. When we share opinions or personal stories, Scripture remains our foundation.

Grace & truth

Following Jesus is both honest and hopeful. We aim to speak truth with kindness and to welcome people where they are.

Practical discipleship

Preach the Gospel every day of your life and if necessary, use words. NOTE We are to live out our faith in Jesus first by our actions and share the word of the Gospel at appropriate times and seasons.

Servant-hearted ministry

We want to love people well, encourage the Church, and help others take steps toward Jesus.

What we believe

In short, we believe:

  • God is the Creator—holy, loving, and worthy of worship.
  • Jesus Christ is the Son of God, fully God and fully man, and the Savior of the world.
  • Salvation is by grace through faith in Jesus—not earned by works.
  • The Bible is God’s Word and is trustworthy for teaching and guidance.
  • God's Holy Spirit is an outflowing that empowers believers to grow in holiness, love, and obedience.

If you’re exploring faith, we’d love to help you take a next step and answer questions.

How to get started

  1. Pick one thing—music, a sermon, or an article.
  2. Take notes on what stood out and why.
  3. Pray and ask God for a single next step to practice this week.
  4. Reach out if you’d like prayer or have questions.

About this quote

The quote is most often attributed to Saint Francis of Assisi, but it is often disputed. Some say that he said “Preach the Gospel every day and also use words.” Others say he didn't say anything of the sort.

No matter the source, the statement says that we should live out our faith through actions and if our actions are not inline, words can never replace it.
