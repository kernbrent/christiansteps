Have You Ever Fed Jesus?

Jesus says that what we do for the overlooked and hurting is done to Him. That truth changes how we see people—and how we love.

Jesus once asked His followers to consider something startling: when you care for the hungry, the thirsty, the stranger, the sick, and the imprisoned, you are caring for Him.

In Matthew 25, the King welcomes the righteous into His kingdom and explains why: “I was hungry and you gave me something to eat… I was a stranger and you invited me in.” When they ask when this ever happened, He replies, “Whatever you did for one of the least of these… you did for me.”

The challenge is not only to help, but to see. It’s easy to reduce people to categories—“the homeless,” “the poor,” “refugees”—and in doing so, quietly take their faces away. But people have names. People have stories. People have dignity.

Sometimes God teaches this with a tiny moment: a person in need refusing a drink because it’s the “wrong color.” At first it can feel ungrateful—until we realize what it reveals: this is not an object of charity. This is a human being, made in God’s image, who simply wants a red juice box.

Love doesn’t say, “Be grateful for what I decided to give you.” Love honors a person. Love listens. Love keeps a face on the “least of these.” And when we love that way, we are closer to Jesus than we realize.
