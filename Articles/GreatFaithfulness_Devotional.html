Great Is Thy Faithfulness

A devotional reflection

Hymn: Great Is Thy Faithfulness

Great is Thy faithfulness, O God my Father;
There is no shadow of turning with Thee;
Thou changest not, Thy compassions, they fail not;
As Thou hast been Thou forever wilt be.

Refrain:
Great is Thy faithfulness!
Great is Thy faithfulness!
Morning by morning new mercies I see;
All I have needed Thy hand hath provided;
Great is Thy faithfulness, Lord, unto me!

A woman on the streets of Greece

On a quiet street in Athens, a homeless woman stood waiting. When food was offered to her, she carefully examined it—not with entitlement, but with dignity. She was not asking for more, only something specific. She wanted a red juice box.

In that small moment, something profound was revealed. She was not invisible. She was not an object of charity. She was a person with preferences, desires, and a story. A human being created in the image of God.

That realization changes everything. When we truly see people—especially the poor, the broken, and the overlooked—we begin to understand what Jesus meant when He said, “Whatever you did for one of the least of these, you did for Me.”

Faithfulness revealed in ordinary moments

God’s faithfulness is rarely announced with thunder. More often, it is discovered in quiet obedience and uncomfortable compassion. It is found when we choose presence over convenience and love over assumption.

The hymn reminds us that God does not change. His compassions never fail. His mercies are not rationed out based on worthiness. They are new every morning.

When we serve others, we are not bringing God somewhere He has not been. We are stepping into the places where He already dwells.

Have you fed Jesus?

Jesus identifies Himself with the hungry, the thirsty, the stranger, the sick, and the imprisoned. This is not poetry—it is theology. To love them is to love Him.

Feeding Jesus does not require wealth or eloquence. Sometimes it looks like honoring a preference. Sometimes it looks like listening. Sometimes it looks like seeing a face where we once saw none.

The faithfulness of God invites a response. Not fear. Not guilt. But love.

Morning by morning, new mercies await.
