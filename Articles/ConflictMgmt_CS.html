Case Study 1: Aligning Expectations in a Church Leadership Transition

Takeaway: Clear, shared expectations—established through structured dialogue—can quickly reduce tension and rebuild trust during leadership transitions.

A major church denomination engaged our services to design a structured approach for addressing leadership conflict during pastoral transitions. The immediate opportunity involved a local congregation that had recently called a new pastor into a long-standing ministry role.

The congregation, staff, and Human Resources Committee carried deeply rooted expectations shaped by previous leadership styles, cultural norms, and informal practices developed over many years. The newly appointed pastor entered the role with a different leadership philosophy, communication style, and understanding of responsibilities.

As tensions increased, misunderstandings began to affect staff morale, decision-making processes, and relational trust. Rather than allowing frustration to escalate into division, the denomination engaged us to create a facilitated process to surface assumptions, clarify expectations, and rebuild alignment while being the preferred process for all local churches within the denomination.

We worked individually and collectively with the pastor, staff members, and HR Committee to identify explicit and implicit expectations. Through guided conversations, facilitated listening sessions, and documented role clarification, each group gained insight into both their own assumptions and those held by others.

The process resulted in a shared framework for leadership expectations, communication rhythms, and decision authority. More importantly, it created space for healing, mutual respect, and renewed trust. The pastor and staff developed a healthier working relationship that continues to support effective ministry and congregational stability.

Case Study 2: Restoring Clarity and Trust in a Small Business Leadership Change

Takeaway: Direct conversations and documented role clarity turn performance conflict into practical alignment that preserves relationships and stabilizes the business.

A small subscription-based online services company requested assistance during a leadership transition involving a newly appointed president and an experienced sales professional.

The new president perceived that the sales role was no longer producing sufficient value relative to compensation. At the same time, the sales professional, who was approaching retirement, felt misunderstood, undervalued, and uncertain about evolving expectations. Communication between the two had become strained, indirect, and increasingly unproductive.

We facilitated structured conversations that allowed both parties to clearly articulate concerns, priorities, and personal constraints. By establishing a neutral environment and clear communication guidelines, grievances were expressed directly rather than through assumption or frustration.

As clarity emerged, the president was able to define revised expectations aligned with the company’s current needs. The sales professional, recognizing changing capacity and personal goals, chose to reduce compensation and delivery commitments accordingly. This mutual realignment preserved the relationship while restoring operational clarity.

In parallel, we worked with the former president to document sales role expectations and performance benchmarks. This documentation provided continuity, reduced ambiguity, and helped the organization avoid similar conflicts with other sales professionals on staff.

This case study demonstrates that unresolved conflict often stems not from ill intent, but from misaligned expectations and unspoken assumptions. When addressed thoughtfully and directly, conflict becomes an opportunity for renewed trust, clarity, and long-term success.

Case Study 3: Addressing Workplace Conduct and Restoring Professional Trust

Takeaway: When conduct concerns are handled promptly with training, coaching, and accountability, teams can regain respect and collaborate effectively again.

In a supervisory role, I managed two direct reports whose working relationship had become strained. One employee raised concerns that her colleague’s communication and behavior toward her felt dismissive and disrespectful, and she believed this treatment was influenced by gender dynamics.

Given the seriousness of the concern, I initiated a formal review process. This included listening carefully to the reporting employee’s experiences, examining documented interactions, and assessing workplace conduct against established organizational standards. The review determined that certain behaviors did not meet expectations for professional communication and respect within the organization.

To address the issue constructively, I required the employee whose conduct was in question to complete the organization’s corporate training on gender discrimination and sex-based harassment. This training was paired with multiple documented one-on-one coaching sessions focused on awareness, accountability, and practical behavior change. All sessions were documented and shared with Human Resources to ensure transparency and alignment with company policy.

Throughout these conversations, it became clear that the employee did not fully recognize how his actions were being perceived or the impact they were having on the work environment. With guidance and clear expectations, he demonstrated a willingness to learn, adjust communication patterns, and modify workplace behavior.

At the same time, I met separately with the reporting employee to provide support and to communicate the steps being taken. As changes began to occur, these conversations helped her understand that the process was producing genuine effort and improvement, which allowed trust to gradually rebuild the work place relationship.

Over time, the two employees were able to reestablish a functional and respectful working relationship. The outcome was not achieved by assigning blame to a group or identity, but by addressing specific behaviors, setting clear expectations, and providing structured opportunities for learning and accountability.

This case study highlights the importance of responding promptly and fairly to workplace concerns, maintaining focus on conduct rather than assumptions, and using established processes to foster growth, understanding, and mutual respect within a team.
